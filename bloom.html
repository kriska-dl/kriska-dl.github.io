Bloom: Sustainably Fresh

Duration: 2 Weeks
Role: UX Research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Testing and Visual Design
Tools: Miro, Sketch, Principle
Group: Dajella Overweg, Yanick Juin, Kriska de Lange

Project Overview

Bloom is a leading regional supermarket focused on offering high-quality products and a personalized shopping experience. They have an established network of premium brick & mortar grocery stores conveniently located to service their client base, which exceeds 30% market share in the region. Although Bloom has a long history of success with its stores, the management team is aware that more and more consumers are switching to online grocery shopping due to its flexibility and convenience.

The Challenge

Bloom’s vision is to design a user experience that goes above and beyond the e-commerce marketplace models that Amazon and other competitors (like Walmart) are offering. They want to add value for their customers by finding a way to leverage their successful premium store model and replicate it into a differentiating digital experience.

Our Process

We were only given 2 weeks to complete the research and the interface for this project. To resolve this time constraint, we worked together in an Agile approach. We also used the design thinking methodology to make sure that the final product meets our users' needs.


1 - Research

We started this project by immersing ourselves in the current online grocery experience. We conducted a quick "competitive analysis" to get an idea on the current market of online grocery shopping. We did this by creating an inventory that assesses what we’re looking for in an online grocery store. This helped us see the current trends and different approaches, as well as things that we can improve. We then moved on to crafting surveys and interviews. A daunting task for beginners as it was hard to make sure that we achieve quality results, but we had to learn to trust the process. In such a short time, we received enough data to carry onto the next step. We used affinity mapping to organize and sort the data we received. This also helped us identify trends and pain points which we later translated onto our personas.

2 - The Problem

Through our research and with our personas in mind, we were able to define what we wanted to focus on for our MVP. Our findings showed that people are hesitant to buy their groceries online because they don’t trust that they will receive their items intact and fresh at a date and time that is convenient for them.

People don’t trust that all the items they select will be available and delivered intact & fresh at a date and time that is convenient for them.

For me, this was one of the most difficult parts of the project - to put our findings in one simple statement. However, once we were able to frame it, our vision became much clearer.

3 - The Solution

After defining our problem and getting to know our users' pain points, we moved on to ideation. We used different tools and exercises to turn these pain points into design opportunities. We wanted to solve many problems but we can only pick one for our MVP. We picked one that which we thought is the most differentiating experience if ever it comes to life. Our solution is to give customers the option to select fresh produce products based on its expiry date. So a product that has a shelf life of a week will be offered on a regular price while the other with a shorter life (ex: 3 days) will be offered on a lower discounted price.

4 - Wireframes

After conducting feature prioritization methods, we designed our wireframes. We also tested our product with some colleagues to get feedback. Without much time, we’ve re-iterated some of the designs & flow along the way.

5 - Visual Design

Our original design has evolved enormously after learning from the feedback we’ve received on our initial presentation. However, we kept the brand attributes moving forward.

Bloom is Modern, Calm, Clean and Fresh.

MVP

- Ability to check available delivery times and reserve from the beginning when you click on the calendar icon

- Personal Promotions are highlighted on the home screen when user is logged in

- Sort products by relevance, recently added, price ascending and price descending.

- Ability to filter products based on brand, type, discount and previous purchases.

- Different price points will be shown from the product page. Green represents the price for the latest stock and the pink shade represents the price for the product with a shorter life span.

- Ability to choose your product based on its expiry date.

- Similar items from the same brand will be featured.

- Suggestions based on the customer’s selection will also be displayed.

- Product description and Nutrition facts for customers that need more information about the product

6 - Future steps

In theory, we plan to continue testing our product and slowly bring in more improvements to our users’ experience. We’re very curious about how our special feature will be received in real life as we definitely considered the challenges on the supply chain and store operations.

7 - Updates

A month after completing this project, we found a similar type of offering being tested at one of the major grocery brands in The Netherlands - Albert Heijn.

It was a proud moment for my group. Although it is still being tested, we had a similar idea and it felt like our research was validated.
